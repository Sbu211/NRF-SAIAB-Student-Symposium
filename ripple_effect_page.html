Key Speakers

Professor R. Dorrington

Professor Dorrington is based in the Department of Biochemistry and Microbiology at Rhodes University. She is a molecular microbiologist leading a large, multidisciplinary research group. Her research interests intersect with biodiversity, microbial symbiosis, and marine natural products with a strong emphasis on both fundamental as well as applied research. Her research explores how marine symbiotic bacterial communities produce bioactive compounds which include promising leads against antimicrobial resistant pathogens.

Professor Anusha Rajkaran

Professor Anusha Rajkaran is based in the Department of Biodiversity and Conservation Biology at the University of the Western Cape. Her research explores the functioning of estuarine habitats, including salt marshes and seagrass, the ecosystem services they provide, and the impacts of microplastic and chemical pollutants. With work spanning much of South Africa’s coastline, she leads a dynamic research team of postgraduate students and postdoctoral fellows focused on understanding and protecting these vital ecosystems.

About the Symposium

NRF-SAIAB welcomes you to the 7th Annual NRF-SAIAB Student Symposium themed “The Ripple Effect: Impactful Science”. This symposium showcases the aquatic research conducted by our student body and provides a platform of our emerging scientists to present their research findings and engage with peers and experts across disciplines. This symposium highlights postgraduate research that not only advances scientific understanding of aquatic ecosystems but also demonstrates potential for real-world impact - whether through informing conservation strategies, shaping policy, or engaging local communities. By fostering academic exchange and collaboration, the symposium supports the development of the next generation of scientists committed to the sustainability of our planet’s oceans, rivers, estuaries, and freshwater systems.
